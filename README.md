# 🗽 EO50 Policy Analysis: Economic Inequality Across NYC Boroughs

Public policy effectiveness depends on understanding where impact is strongest — this project uses geospatial and temporal data visualization to analyze how EO50 certifications are distributed across New York City and what this reveals about economic inequality and policy reach.

---

## 🚨 The Problem

Policies aimed at promoting equal employment and economic growth often fail to reach all communities equally. Without a clear understanding of how these initiatives are distributed across regions and socio-economic conditions, gaps in implementation remain hidden.

This leads to:
- uneven policy adoption across boroughs  
- lower participation in economically weaker areas  
- reduced overall effectiveness of public initiatives  

---

## 💡 The Solution

This project integrates multiple datasets to visualize and analyze the distribution of EO50 certifications across NYC boroughs, combining spatial and temporal perspectives.

By linking certification data with economic indicators such as employment rates, poverty levels, and median income, the analysis provides a clearer picture of how policy adoption varies across different socio-economic environments.

---

## 📊 Business Impact

The insights highlight structural disparities in policy reach and provide a foundation for more targeted and effective interventions.

This enables:
- better allocation of public resources  
- targeted outreach in underrepresented areas  
- improved policy design and sustainability  
- stronger alignment between economic conditions and policy impact  

---

## 🛠️ How it was done

A geospatial visualization was used to map certificate distribution across boroughs, revealing that areas with higher employment rates tend to have higher certification density, while regions with higher poverty rates show lower participation (as seen in the spatial plot on page 3) :contentReference[oaicite:1]{index=1}.

A temporal analysis further examined how certification trends evolved between 2021 and 2024, highlighting a significant spike in 2022 followed by a decline, suggesting challenges in sustaining compliance over time (page 4) :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}.

These insights were combined to develop policy recommendations focused on improving outreach, resource allocation, and long-term sustainability.
